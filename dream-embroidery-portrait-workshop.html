Why This Style of Embroidery Looks Much Harder Than It Really Is

You see a portrait like this and your first thought is probably:

"There's no way I could ever make something like that."

It doesn't look like traditional embroidery. The soft thread, flowing textures, and detailed portraits make it seem like something only highly skilled artists can create.

But what most people don't realize is that these portraits aren't stitched all at once.

They're built gradually.

One section at a time.
One layer at a time.
One simple stitch at a time.

What looks complicated in the finished piece becomes surprisingly manageable when the process is broken down into small steps.

Instead of trying to create an entire portrait at once, you simply focus on the next section in front of you.

And before long, the portrait begins to take shape.
