You see a portrait like this and your first thought is probably:

"There's no way I could ever make something like that."

But what most people don't realize is that these portraits aren't stitched all at once.

They're built gradually.

One section at a time.
One layer at a time.
One simple stitch at a time.

What looks complicated in the finished piece becomes surprisingly manageable when the process is broken down into small steps.

Instead of trying to create an entire portrait at once, you simply focus on the next section in front of you.

And before long, the portrait begins to take shape.

Here's a Small Taste of What's Inside:

Module 1:

Getting Ready: Setting Up Your Hoop, Fabric & Thread

Before you make a single stitch, let's get your setup right. This is the part most beginners rush and later wish they hadn't. We'll keep it simple and get you ready the right way.

  • How to stretch your black fabric in the hoop nice and tight so every stitch sits flat and clean
  • Why black fabric makes your thread colours glow with warmth and depth against the dark background
  • The easy way to copy your portrait outline onto the dark fabric using a white chalk pencil
  • How to set up your thread colours so you're ready to stitch without stopping to untangle
  • You'll finish ready to go: hoop set up, fabric in place, outline ready to trace

Module 2:

Transfer the Full Design. No Drawing Required

You don't need to be an artist to get a beautiful design onto your fabric. This module walks you through tracing the full portrait step by step. The template guides your hand the whole way.

  • The simple trace-and-transfer method using a white chalk pencil (no freehand drawing required)
  • How to trace the woman's profile with smooth, clean lines (the template does the hard work)
  • How to trace the flowing hair, tall candle, and stars so the full design is ready before you stitch
  • You'll finish with every line in place, relaxed, prepared, and ready to start stitching

Module 3:

Stitching the Face: Bringing the Skin to Life

This is the moment your first real stitches go in. You'll fill the face with warm, layered skin tones and watch it slowly lift off the dark background. It's the part that makes you realise this is actually happening.

  • How to stitch the face smoothly so the skin looks soft, warm, and full of life
  • The easy way to layer peach, copper, and warm brown tones for a natural, glowing look
  • How to follow the contours of the face so it looks round and real, not flat
  • Simple tips to keep your stitches neat and even as you go
  • You'll finish with a beautiful completed face glowing against the black background. Your first big win.

Module 4:

Stitching the Hair: Adding Fire and Movement

Now the portrait really comes alive. Long, twisted stitches in deep reds, burnt oranges, and rich crimsons fill almost the entire hoop with colour and movement.

  • How to create the twisted, rope-like stitch style that gives the hair its dramatic flowing texture
  • The easy way to layer deep red, orange, and crimson tones for a rich, fiery look
  • How to stitch the hair so it flows outward and fills the hoop with movement
  • How to let the gold stars show through between the strands as you work
  • You'll finish with the face and hair complete. The portrait will already look stunning.

Module 5:

The Final Touches: Stitching the Candle & Completing Your Portrait

This is the finish line. In this last module you'll stitch the glowing candle that brings the whole portrait together. Warm golds, deep ambers, and a softly lit flame. When the candle is done, so is your portrait. And it will take your breath away.

  • How to stitch the candle body so it looks solid, warm, and textured
  • The easy way to blend gold, amber, and tan tones for a richly lit candle
  • How to stitch the flame so it looks like it's genuinely glowing against the dark background
  • How to finish and secure your threads so everything looks clean and polished
  • The last steps: tidy up your work, step back, and take in what you've made

A: None at all. The workshop is built for complete beginners. It starts from the very first stitch, explains every decision as you go, and assumes you've never held a needle before. If you can thread a needle and make a simple loop, you can do this.

Q: How long does it take to complete the portrait?

A: Most students finish in a few relaxed sessions spread over one to two weeks, working at a pace that feels comfortable. There's no deadline and no pressure to rush. The workshop will be waiting exactly where you left it whenever you come back.

Q: What materials do I need?

A: Just a few basics: a wooden embroidery hoop, black fabric, embroidery thread in the right colours, and a needle. Module 1 walks you through exactly what to buy and where to find it, so you won't waste money on the wrong things.

Q: My stitches always come out messy. Will mine actually look like the portrait in the photo?

A: This is the most common worry, and it's exactly what this workshop is designed to fix. Messy stitches almost always come down to tension and direction, not talent. Once I show you the simple tension technique in Module 1, things start clicking. And black fabric is far more forgiving than most materials beginners work with.
